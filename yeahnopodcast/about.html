About the Podcast:

Yeah No Yeah is the fat brain baby of two very old men trapped in the bodies of two sort-of young women: Molly Kendrick and Katie Joy. Molly met Katie when they both lived in Austin and shared a co-working space. When they first met, Katie said, “Here’s who I don’t like,” and Molly knew she had a new best friend, and also that she really missed the East Coast.

At the time, Molly was dating an audio engineer, so it was only natural that they put all that sound equipment to some use. They started a (very embarrassing) podcast called “Ask the Meatloaf Twins.” (Molly loves making meatloaf, Katie loves Meatloaf the singer. It was based on a couples’ Halloween costume that never came to be.) After 11 episodes, Molly and the audio engineer broke up, so they sought a fresh start with Yeah No Yeah and one of Austin’s finest part-time sound guys, Chris (who made our intro and made us believe in love again).

Season 1 of Yeah No Yeah focused on advice, but after 19 episodes — and a brush with local fame when they hosted two live episodes at Dozen Street — they decided they were tired of badgering friends for questions. Katie moved back to New York City to run her company and Molly conveniently decided she was done with Texas forever. Now they’re ready to hear your ghost stories, favorite folklore, urban legends, and scarier Tinder dates: yeahnopodcast@gmail.com.

The Stars:

Katie

Katie Joy is a self proclaimed Instagram celebrity (@whenyouyourowndaddy) and proud dye job living in Jersey City with her two sweet babies: Lux Interior & Chilton. When she isn't drowning in gin at a YOUR local dive bar, you can find her asking strangers 'what kind of porn are you into?'. Her favorite ghost stories are ones she has witnessed herself and her favorite material is leather.

Molly

Molly Kendrick is a freelance writer based in Brooklyn. She writes a humor blog called Contemptible Impudence that you can find at MollyKendrick.com. You’ll find her haunting co-working spaces and coffee shops as she scowls into her laptop trying to think of decent pitches for magazines while working part-time for a travel website. Molly doesn’t believe in ghosts, but loves listening to people exaggerate while arching her eyebrows and taking long, dubious sips of her martini.
